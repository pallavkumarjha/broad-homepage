Legal / Protocol

Terms of the Road.

§ 01 — THE OPEN ROAD

Scope of Service.

Broad is a companion, not a commander. By using this application, you acknowledge that you are operating a motor vehicle on public or private roads at your own risk. Broad provides data, telemetry, and tools for communication, but the responsibility for safe operation lies entirely with the rider.
§ 02 — THE SOS MECHANISM

Emergency Protocol.

The SOS feature is designed to transmit your last known coordinates to your designated convoy or emergency contacts. Broad does not guarantee delivery of these messages in areas without cellular connectivity, though we attempt to queue messages for transmission once signal returns. This is not a replacement for professional satellite emergency beacons (PLBs).
§ 03 — DATA & PRIVACY

The Telemetry.

We collect high-frequency location data to power the "Convoy" and "SOS" features. This data is transmitted encrypted and is only visible to the riders you explicitly invite to your group. For detailed information on our handling of your telemetry, see our Privacy Policy.
§ 04 — GLOVEBOX CONTENT

Digital Originals.

Documents stored in the Broad Glovebox are AES-256 encrypted and stored locally on your device. While Broad helps you manage these digital copies, you are responsible for ensuring they are valid and for carrying physical originals where required by local law.
§ 05 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

The Long Way Home.

Broad, its workshop, and its contributors shall not be held liable for any accidents, injuries, or mechanical failures occurring during a ride. We build tools for riders because we are riders, but the road remains an unpredictable place. Ride within your limits.
