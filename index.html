§ 01 — A Note from the Workshop

The best rides aren't planned in spreadsheets. They happen when your crew shows up, the weather holds, and the phone stays in your pocket. Broad is the app we wished existed — quiet enough to disappear, sharp enough to save a life.

§ 02 — What Broad Does

Four instruments. One ride.

— 01 / Trip Planner

Routes, drawn by riders.

Plot a trip, pin the stops that matter, invite your crew. Broad turns scattered group-chat threads into a single, living itinerary — with waypoints, ETAs, and the good roads marked.

  • Custom Waypoints ∞
  • Group Invites One Tap
  • Offline Maps Included
  • Curated Routes 400+
— 02 / The Red Button

An SOS that actually shows up.

One tap sends your location, heading, and last-known speed to every rider in your convoy — and to emergency contacts if you're riding solo. No signal? Broad queues it and sends the moment bars return.

  • Trigger Time < 300ms
  • Offline Queue Enabled
  • Auto-Crash Detect Gyro + GPS
  • Emergency Contacts Up to 5

— 03 / The Convoy

Everyone on the map. Always.

Live location, live speed, live fuel — for every rider in your group. See who's ahead, who pulled over, who needs to catch up. No more yelling into helmets or waiting at the next petrol pump, wondering.

  • Location Refresh 2s Interval
  • Per-Rider Stats Speed · Fuel · Battery
  • Voice Check-In Hands Free
  • Convoy Size Up to 24
— 04 / The Glovebox

Papers, kept precisely.

Licence, registration, insurance, PUC, emergency medical — all in one place, encrypted, offline-ready. When the constable waves you down or the tyre blows at midnight, everything you need is two taps away.

  • Encryption AES-256
  • Offline Access Always
  • Expiry Reminders Automatic
  • Document Slots Unlimited

§ 03 — Field Entry

A small story.

It was late. The ghat roads had emptied out and we'd lost Rhea somewhere after the last fuel stop — the kind of lost that turns a good ride bad. In the old days we'd have turned around, shouted into three bars of signal, made a mess of it. Instead, someone opened Broad. There she was, a small amber dot, pulled over exactly eleven kilometres back, helmet off, waving for a tow. We found her in twenty minutes. We were home by midnight. That's the whole pitch.

— Entry 014 Spiti Valley · October
